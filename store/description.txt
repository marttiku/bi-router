Plan cycling and walking routes using Strava's global heatmap. See where people actually ride and run, then plan your own route and navigate it on your phone.

FEATURES

• Strava heatmap overlay — see popular cycling, running, water, and winter sport routes with adjustable color and opacity
• Click-to-route planning — add waypoints by clicking the map; routes auto-calculate via BRouter
• Tallinn bicycle roads — toggle official city bike infrastructure data
• GPX export — download your route for any GPS device or app
• Google Maps integration — open your route in Google Maps with one click
• Mobile navigation via QR code — scan to open a GPS-tracked navigation page on your phone, no app needed
• Route stats — live distance and estimated time with adjustable speed

HOW IT WORKS

1. Log into Strava and visit the global heatmap page
2. Click the "Plan Route" button (or click the extension icon anytime)
3. Click the map to add waypoints — the route calculates automatically
4. Export as GPX, open in Google Maps, or tap "Send to Phone" and scan the QR code

MOBILE NAVIGATION

The QR code opens a lightweight navigation page directly in your phone's browser. No app install, no file downloads. You get:
• Live GPS position on the map
• Distance remaining and ETA
• Direction compass
• Screen wake lock

Works best in Tallinn and surrounding areas with the bike roads layer, but route planning works worldwide wherever Strava has heatmap data.

PERMISSIONS

• Cookies — reads Strava session cookies to authenticate heatmap tile requests
• DeclarativeNetRequest — attaches auth headers to heatmap tile requests
• Host permissions for strava.com and gis.tallinn.ee
